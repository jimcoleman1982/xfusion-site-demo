How To Align Sales and Customer Success

2026-05-05 · 6 min read

How to align sales and customer success

With SaaS, aligning sales and customer success (CS) teams is crucial for improving communication, efficiency, and business growth. Sales teams are responsible for acquiring new customers and closing deals, while CS teams focus on keeping customers happy and ensuring their success.

When these two teams work together effectively, they can drive higher retention, recurring revenue, and opportunities for cross-sells and upsells.

Define segments and personas

Understanding ideal customers

Defining client segments, personas, and customer success goals is the first step in aligning the sales and customer success teams.

Knowing who your ideal clients are enables both parties to focus their efforts more successfully. Sales may concentrate on obtaining the proper consumers by identifying the essential traits and requirements of your target audience, and customer success can customize their assistance to address the particular requirements of those clients.

For instance, developing thorough customer profiles including demographic data, pain areas, and desired results may help sales and customer success teams better understand their target audience and how to engage with them.

Involve customer success in the sales process

Providing comprehensive support

Early customer success team involvement in the sales process guarantees that clients receive full assistance from the outset. By working together, CS teams are better equipped to comprehend customer expectations and give the sales team insightful information that improves the customer experience as a whole.

Customer success managers, for instance, might take part in sales calls to offer further details and respond to inquiries concerning the onboarding and support procedures, painting a clear image for prospective clients of what to anticipate.

Develop a handover process

Ensuring seamless transition

Continuity and a smooth transition for the client depend on a clearly defined handover process between sales and customer success.

Customers want information to be exchanged across teams in high-touch business models, therefore creating a disciplined handover procedure is essential.

For instance, the customer success team may take over more smoothly and consistently if a common document containing client information, expectations, and any promises made throughout the sales process is implemented.

Schedule regular check-ins

Maintaining alignment

Frequent check-ins between the customer success, marketing, and sales teams support alignment and collaboration by giving everyone a chance to discuss progress, exchange insights, and resolve any issues that may come up.

For instance, meetings can be scheduled on a weekly or biweekly basis to review important metrics, talk about customer feedback, and plan collaborative initiatives that improve the customer experience.

Create a customer journey map

Mapping the customer experience

Making a map of the customer journey is an effective way to get the customer success and sales teams in sync.

Offering a thorough overview of the customer lifecycle, this map describes the client's journey from first awareness to after-purchase assistance.

A customer journey map, for instance, may have phases for awareness, consideration, purchase, onboarding, and retention, emphasizing the functions of sales and customer success at each level.

Including all teams

By incorporating all pertinent teams into the customer journey map, all departments will be in alignment and able to deliver a smooth and satisfying journey for the customer as a result of effective team collaboration.

For instance, the customer journey map can be a useful tool for maintaining alignment since it makes clear how various teams collaborate and assigns metrics to each stage.

Forward customer feedback

Sharing insights with stakeholders

Sharing user insights with sales and customer success teams enables them to make well-informed decisions that improve customer experience and spur business growth.

For instance, putting in place a system for gathering and disseminating customer feedback, such as frequent surveys and feedback forms, ensures that insightful information is shared with all pertinent teams and is vital for continuous improvement.

Foster a collaborative environment

Efficient communication is the key to aligning sales and customer success teams. Open channels of communication across these two teams allow for the best possible service, increased customer value, and reduced churn over time.

By defining segments and personas, involving customer success in the sales process, developing a handover process, scheduling regular check-ins, creating a customer journey map, and forwarding customer feedback, businesses can foster a collaborative environment that benefits both the organization and its customers.

Aligning sales and customer success is not just a strategy but a commitment to creating a unified approach to customer experience. Implement these tips to ensure that your sales and customer success teams are working together effectively, driving growth, and enhancing customer satisfaction.
