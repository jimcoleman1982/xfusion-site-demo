Best Practices for Sending Product Update Emails to SaaS Customers

2026-05-05 · 6 min read

Best practices for sending product update emails to SaaS customers

Product update emails are educational and critical to any SaaS firm. They inform your customers about modifications in the products that you offer. Regular updates keep the users engaged with the product, refresh their memory on the perceived value you added to your product, and assure the customer that you are constantly working towards enhancing their experience.

A study conducted by Bain & Company shows that "improving customer retention rates by 5% boosts profit by 25-95%," which explains why it is paramount always to update them.

Key elements of effective product update emails

To craft effective product update emails, consider incorporating the following key elements:

  • Clear and concise messaging: Make sure that your main point is precise. The customer should be able to immediately get an overall view of the product updates.

  • Strong call to action (CTA): Make sure that it has a persuasive headline and a strong CTA telling the customer to check out the enhancements or additions.

  • Visual appeal: Add beautiful graphics and images; this will help to support your message and make the email even more interesting.

  • Customer-centric approach: The releases also need to describe how the changes will improve the user experience rather than simply outlining new features.

Crafting compelling subject lines

The subject line is your first opportunity to capture the user's attention. A compelling subject line can significantly increase open rates and encourage users to engage with your content.

Examples:

  • "Exciting New Features in [Your Product], Check Them Out!"
  • "We've Made Some Updates You'll Love!"
  • "Discover the Latest Enhancements to [Your Product]"

Tips for crafting compelling subject lines:

  • Keep it short and to the point.

  • Use action-oriented language.

  • Avoid using spammy words like "free" or "discount."

Personalizing your product update emails

Every customer is unique, and this is where personalization can make a great difference in the success rate of your product update emails.

Ways to personalize:

  • Use the customer's name: To make the email more friendly it is advisable to address them by their first name.

  • Mention specific usage: Cite instances where the customer has benefited by using or purchasing your product.

  • Offer tailored recommendations: From using the product or service, offer customized recommendations or improvements.

Timing your product updates

It is, therefore, important to consider the appropriate time to send the product update emails. They should be sent at a time when the user will be in a position to receive new information, but will not be overwhelmed with it.

Best practices:

  • Frequency: Ensure that the updates are not too frequent, the updates should be frequent enough to cover developments but not too frequent to be irritating. Quarterly updates are a good start.

  • Event-based: Send emails if there is a new feature release or something important has been done in the software.

  • User behavior: It's crucial to consider the user's activity level to define the best time to send updates.

Designing visually appealing emails

Layout is arguably one of the most important elements when it comes to creating engaging emails. Studies show that "people remember 80% of what they see and only 20% of what they read."

Design tips:

  • Use high-quality images: Adding the right pictures/icons can take the email a notch higher in terms of its aesthetics.

  • Ensure mobile compatibility: Your email should be mobile-responsive, people will likely read emails on their mobile devices.

  • Keep it simple: The consumers must not be burdened with too much information about the product.

  • Brand consistency: Each of your widgets must correspond to the aesthetics and design guide set for your brand.

Highlighting key features and benefits

When launching a new product feature, it is recommended to focus on what you are going to provide or bring to your audience.

Focus on the product's key improvements and innovations such that it addresses their needs and wants.

Tips for highlighting key features:

  • Use bullet points: If the updates are many, summarize them in the form of bullet points to ensure clarity.

  • Include screenshots or videos: Illustrate how the new features will work with the use of models and demonstrations.

  • Provide context: Describe why the changes were made and how they will benefit the users.

Measuring success and optimizing your strategy

Evaluation of your product update emails has to be carried out continuously to determine the success of your emails.

Monitor relevant analytics like open rates, click-through rates, and user engagements to determine what needs to be adjusted.

Tips for measuring and optimizing:

  • A/B testing: Experiment with different subject lines, email messages, and call-to-action to determine which approach gets the most response.

  • Analyze feedback: Administer surveys or polls to get feedback from the users to understand their reactions to the changes.

  • Iterate and improve: Always utilize the data you have collected to try and improve your email strategy.
